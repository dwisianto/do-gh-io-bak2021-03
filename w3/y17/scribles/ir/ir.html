Performance Measures on Ranking Result

Relevance Levels Performance Measures
Binary Precision, Mean Average Precision, and Mean Reciprocal Rank
Multiple levels Normalized Discounted Cumulative Gain

Precision @K

1. Set a rank threshold K
2. Compute the number of relevant documents in top K
3. Ignores documents ranked lower than K
For Example
Suppose a search engine returns five documents.
Relevance documents at position 1,3, and 5.
Irrelevance documents at position 2 and 4.
Prec@1=1/1
Prec@2=1/2
Prec@3=2/3
Prec@4=2/4
Prec@5=3/5

Average precision

Definition Average precision is the average of Precision@K.
Compute Precision@K for all R relevant documents.
- K_1 is the rank position for the first relevant document.
- K_2 is the rank position for the second relevant document.
- K_R is the rank position for the R^{th} relevant document.
Example1
Prec@1=1/1
Prec@2=1/2
Prec@3=2/3
Prec@4=2/4
Prec@5=3/5
AP= 1/3 (1/1+2/3+3/5)
